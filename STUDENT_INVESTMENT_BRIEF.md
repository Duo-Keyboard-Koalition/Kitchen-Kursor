## Is This Platform B2B or B2C?

**Short answer: It's B2C2B — consumer-first, institution-second.**

The platform runs on two parallel tracks that reinforce each other:

```
B2C Layer (students)          B2B Layer (institutions)
─────────────────────         ──────────────────────────────
Students sign up              Loblaw / Loblaw Advance (retail media)
Upload & discover recipes     Universities (food service partnerships)
Add to NoName cart            PC Optimum (loyalty infrastructure)
Earn PCO points               Campus distribution hub operators
Share with peers              CPG brands (sponsored ingredient placement)
```

### Why B2C2B Is the Right Model

| Model | What It Means Here | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| **B2C** | Students are the end user | They generate the content, build the community, and create the demand signal |
| **B2B** | Loblaw and universities are the business partners | They provide the product catalog, loyalty infrastructure, and campus distribution |
| **The flywheel** | More students → more recipes → more cart conversions → more valuable to Loblaw → better deals for students → more students | Each side makes the other stronger |

> **The model is B2C at the front door, B2B at the revenue layer.** Students never pay for the platform. Revenue flows from Loblaw (retail media, cart commissions, data licensing) and university partnerships — unlocked by the consumer engagement that students generate.

This is the same model as:
- **Instacart** — free for users, monetizes through grocery retail partnerships
- **TikTok Shop** — free for creators, monetizes through brand and merchant relationships
- **Flipp** — free for shoppers, monetizes through retailer ad spend

### What Consumers Are Doing About It

Shoppers are not passively absorbing price increases — they are actively restructuring how they buy food:

1. **Brand switching** — 61% of Canadian consumers ready to switch brands for better value
2. **Private label migration** — 42% buying more store-brand items
3. **Loyalty program intensification** — ~50% paying more attention to points and rewards
4. **Fewer impulse purchases** — deliberate, list-driven shopping is up
5. **Recipe-driven shopping** — buying only what a specific recipe requires, eliminating waste-spend

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Current "Solution" | The Problem |
|---|---|

| Meal kit services (HelloFresh) | $10–$15/serving — too expensive; solves convenience, not cost |
| Grocery store loyalty apps | Passive discounts, no inspiration, no community |
| TikTok food content | Inspiring but disconnected from purchase — no checkout integration |
| Campus food banks | Solve hunger in crisis, don't build sustainable food habits or skills |

**The gap:** There is no platform that combines price-optimized recipe discovery + private label ingredient matching + loyalty rewards + community — until now.

### How NoName Recipes Attacks Rising Prices Directly

#### 1. Default to Private Label

Every ingredient match in the platform defaults to the **NoName equivalent first** — the same product, at up to 20% less than national brands. The user never has to hunt for the cheaper option; it's automatically surfaced.

```
Recipe calls for: "2 cups all-purpose flour"
Platform suggests: no name all-purpose flour — $2.49
vs. national brand: $3.29
Per-recipe saving: $0.80
Per-month saving (cooking 5x/week): ~$17
Per-year saving: ~$200 on flour alone
```

#### 2. PCO Points Turn Grocery Spend Into Future Discounts

Every NoName purchase made through the platform earns PC Optimum points. Points are redeemable as cash toward future groceries — effectively a recurring discount on top of private label pricing.

| Behavior | Points Earned |
|---|---|
| Upload a recipe | PCO points |
| Recipe gets 10 likes | PCO bonus |
| Purchase ingredients via cart | Standard PCO earn rate |
| Purchase NoName-brand items | Boosted PCO earn rate (partner incentive) |

#### 3. Budget-Anchored Recipe Discovery

The platform can surface recipes filtered by **cost per serving** — showing students meals they can make for $2, $3, or $5/serving. This transforms "what's for dinner?" from a question of taste into a question of *affordable* taste.

#### 4. Reduce Household Food Waste (Which Is a Hidden Price Driver)

The average person wastes **$762/year** in food — often because they bought ingredients for a recipe, used half, and let the rest expire. NoName Recipes addresses this by:
- Surfacing recipes that use complete package quantities
- Suggesting "use up" recipes based on what a user already has
- Building shopping lists that match exact recipe quantities to available package sizes

**Eliminating food waste is equivalent to a 10–15% grocery price cut** for the average household — with no change in what they pay at checkout.

### The PCO Points Model: Investing in Students Pays Back

The platform invests in students through PCO points — but this investment is self-liquidating:

```
Student uploads recipe
    → Platform awards PCO points (cost: ~$0.10 in points)
    → Recipe gets shared to 50 followers
    → 5 followers view recipe
    → 2 add to NoName cart (avg. basket: $35)
    → Loblaw earns $70 in cart revenue from one $0.10 points investment
    → Platform earns commission on that $70
    → ROI on student investment: 700x+
```

This model means every point awarded to a student is an investment with a quantifiable return — not a cost center.

### The Anti-Stigma Advantage

Existing student food assistance programs fail because they require students to identify as food-insecure — a deeply stigmatizing act that **two-thirds of eligible students refuse to do.**

NoName Recipes reframes the entire proposition:

| Old Frame | New Frame |
|---|---|
| "I need help affording food" | "I'm a foodie who knows how to eat well on a budget" |
| Food bank visit (stigmatized) | Scrolling recipes on an app (social, normal) |
| Charity recipient | Community contributor (recipe uploader, liked creator) |
| Passive aid | Active engagement with rewards |

**The platform reaches students who would never accept help by never framing it as help.**
